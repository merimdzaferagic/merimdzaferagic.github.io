Applied systems

Research projects & technology programs

Leading and contributing to multi-institutional efforts that bridge theory and deployed networks.

6G-XCEL: 6G Trans-Continental Edge Learning (2024–Present)

Developing a decentralized, AI-native framework across radio and optical layers to enable secure, sustainable, and trust-worthy 6G network control. The project creates reference use cases, data and model repositories, and benchmarking for multi-party AI while partnering with researchers across the EU and the US for global validation and standardization.

SoftEdge: Architectures and Algorithms for Software-Defined Edge Systems (2022–2024)

An SFI-funded effort to converge SDN/NFV intelligence with edge computing. We bring machine learning into the control plane so the wireless system reconfigures itself based on demand, while the project also investigates economic models, virtualization, and pricing mechanisms for efficient resource orchestration.

FIREMAN: Rare Event Prediction via Machine learning and IoT (2019–2022)

Led Trinity College Dublin’s contribution to a framework that relies on IoT, big data fusion, and predictive analytics to detect and intervene on rare industrial events. Responsibilities included research coordination, documentation, and milestone delivery across the European consortium.

CreativeConnections, Startup (2020–2022)

Co-founded a startup to simplify the interaction between SMEs and online influencers for marketing campaigns. Work spanned problem definition, customer validation, personas, value proposition, market intelligence, and team development.

NearBy, Startup (2018–2020)

Turned a jointly filed patent into a venture that used distributed intelligence to optimize mobility in networks. Crafted the full business development lifecycle and secured funding for in-depth market research.

Complex Systems Science for Wireless Networks (US Air Force / Science Foundation Ireland, 2017–2018)

Designed networks with degeneracy-driven self-healing and resilience capabilities, overseeing the science effort for the project.

BH Telecom Configuration and Analysis Platform (2014–2015)

Architected a SaaS-based multi-vendor platform for radio access network configuration and analytics. Built independent services to ensure scalability and flexibility under dynamic conditions.

Smart TCU/SIU Configuration Generator (2014)

Delivered a customized Porteus Linux solution that automated configuration file generation for Ericsson TCU/SIU devices, simplifying deployment for BH Mobile assembly teams.

Tender expert evaluations and telecom design (2011–2013)

Served on expert panels for digital microwave procurement and DVB-T receiver specification in Bosnia and Herzegovina, while also designing telecom networks for Koridor 5C tunnel and implementing an embedded VoIP solution for Iskratel’s InnBox F68 device.

Ericsson Mobile Health system (2012)

Collaborated on the prototype, defining and testing health measurements, database design, and validation workflows.
